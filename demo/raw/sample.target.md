# Volume 1, Chapter 1: Meeting the Angel

"What are you doing?"

The first time Fujimiya Amane spoke to her—Mahiru Shiina—was when he saw her sitting on a park swing in the drizzling rain.

After entering high school this year, Amane had started living alone. In the apartment room to his right lived an angel.

Of course, calling her an angel was just a metaphor, but the girl named Mahiru Shiina was so beautiful and lovely that the metaphor felt almost real.

Her well-maintained, long, straight, ash-blonde hair was always smooth and glossy. Her translucent, milky-white skin was flawless, without a single rough patch.

Her nose was straight and refined, her large eyes framed by long, delicate lashes, and her lips were full, glossy, and beautifully shaped—a cherry-pink that seemed almost artificially perfect.

Amane and she attended the same high school and were in the same grade, so he often heard rumors about her. Most described her as a "beautiful honor student excelling in both academics and sports."

She consistently ranked first in exams and performed at the top 级别 in physical education. Since they were in different classes, Amane didn't know much about her, but if the rumors were true, she was truly a perfect superhuman.

She seemed to have no obvious flaws. She was beautiful, intelligent, yet never arrogant about her achievements. Her personality was humble and composed. It was no wonder she was so popular.

Living next door to such an incredible beauty must have made some boys envious, wishing they could be in the same situation.

That said, Amane had no intention of getting involved with her, nor did he think he'd ever have the chance.

In his eyes, the girl named Mahiru Shiina was certainly charming.

But they were just neighbors. Amane had no opportunity to speak with her and no desire to get entangled.

After all, getting involved would likely invite jealousy from other boys. Besides, if simply living next door made it easy to get close to her, the boys who admired her wouldn't have to work so hard.

For the record, feeling attracted to the opposite sex didn't necessarily mean romantic feelings. To Amane, Mahiru was simply a beautiful girl best admired from a distance.

Thus, he held no expectations of developing any sweet, romantic relationship with her and made no effort to approach her. He just lived next door to her, never interacting.

So when he happened to see her standing alone in the rain without an umbrella, looking lost, his only thought was, "What is she doing?" and he gave her a suspicious look.

The rain was heavy enough that everyone was rushing home, yet she sat alone on a swing in the park between the school and the apartment.

(Why is she sitting there getting soaked?)

The sky was covered in dark gray clouds, blocking the sunlight and casting the surroundings in gloom. The torrential rain that had been falling since morning made the view even blurrier. Still, her conspicuous ash-blonde hair and uniform made it easy for Amane to recognize her as Mahiru.

He couldn't understand why she wasn't using an umbrella, just sitting there, drenched.

She didn't seem to be waiting for anyone, nor did she appear bothered by being wet. She just stared blankly in some direction.

Her slightly upturned face, already unusually pale, looked even more unwell—almost sickly.

Even in a state where she'd likely catch a cold if unlucky, Mahiru sat there quietly.

Since she didn't seem to want to go home, she must have chosen to stay in the rain. Maybe it wasn't his place to interfere.

Thinking this, Amane was about to pass through the park—but then he saw Mahiru's expression, her face crumpling as if she was about to cry. He couldn't help but scratch his head.

He had no intention of getting involved with her.

But leaving someone with that expression alone felt wrong. That was all.

"...What are you doing?"

To show he meant no harm, Amane spoke in the coldest tone he could muster. Then, her long, rain-heavy hair swayed as she turned toward him.

But leaving someone with that expression alone felt wrong. That was all.

Her face was still beautiful.

Even soaked by the rain, her radiance wasn't dimmed; instead, the rain seemed to enhance her beauty, like a prop highlighting her lovely features. It was probably what people called "dewy and alluring."

Her large, double-lidded eyes looked at him, glistening.

Mahiru vaguely knew Amane was her neighbor—they occasionally passed each other in the morning.

But because he suddenly spoke to her, and someone she never interacted with had approached her, her caramel-colored eyes reflected a hint of wariness.

"Fujimiya-kun, do you need something?" Amane thought, "So she remembers my surname," while sensing she wouldn't let her guard down.

Though they weren't complete strangers, it was understandable that she'd be cautious when an unfamiliar neighbor spoke to her.

She probably didn't want to get involved with guys. He'd heard that boys from all grades often confessed to her or tried to approach her, so she likely thought he had ulterior motives too.

"Nothing much. I just saw you sitting here alone in the heavy rain. Anyone would be concerned."

"I see. Thank you for your concern, but I'm here because I want to be. Please don't worry about me."

Her voice wasn't sharp or overtly wary, just gentle but firm, leaving no room for further inquiry.

(Sigh, I knew it.)

She clearly had some reason, but since she was rejecting his concern, Amane decided not to press further.

He'd only spoken to her on a whim, and asking the reason was just incidental. He wasn't that invested.

If she wanted to stay, that was fine. From Mahiru's perspective, Amane's behavior probably made her wonder, "Why is he talking to me?"

She studied him, her delicate, beautiful face tinged with suspicion, so Amane simply replied, "I see."

Pestering her would definitely make her dislike him. It was time to back off.

Fortunately, whether Mahiru's impression of him was good or bad didn't matter—they had nothing to do with each other. He could decisively leave her alone and go home.

Still, leaving a girl alone to get soaked in the rain made him feel a bit guilty.

"You'll catch a cold. Take your umbrella and go home. You don't have to refuse."

So, in the end, Amane meddled a little more.

If she got sick, he'd probably feel uneasy eating and sleeping. With that thought, he handed her the umbrella he'd been holding.

After making her take the umbrella—or rather, forcing it into her hands—Amane turned and left before she could speak.

As he walked away quickly, he heard Mahiru's voice from behind.

Her voice was so faint it was almost drowned out by the rain, but Amane kept moving, swiftly crossing the park.

After forcing his umbrella on her, thinking, "I hope she doesn't catch a cold," he felt like he'd somewhat eased the guilt he'd felt for initially wanting to ignore her and walk away.

Since she refused to talk, Amane had no intention of interacting with her again.

They clearly had no connection. There wouldn't be a next time.

At least, that's what Amane thought as he headed home again.
